Dicey Dungeon

What You Need

  • Full DnD Dice Set: D4, D6, D8, D10, D12, D20, D100
  • Square grid map or graph paper
  • Pencil/pen

How to Play

Step 1: Roll All Dice

Roll all 7 dice together: D4, D6, D8, D10, D12, D20, and D100. Each one guides how you build the dungeon.

Step 2: Draw the Hallway

Use the D4 result divided by 2 (round up) to determine how long to draw the hallway (in squares). Draw it in any direction.

Step 3: Number of Doors

  • D6 รท 2 (round up): This roll gives you the total number of doors or exits for the room.

Step 4: Add Exits

Use the D4 result (full value) to determine which walls have exits. Count clockwise from the entrance:

  • Example: If 2 is rolled, exits are on walls 2 and 3.

Place exits in the middle of the wall, adjusting for fit. Roll D10 to determine if an exit is locked or hidden.

Step 5: Create a Room

  • D10 result gives the room length.
  • D100 result (first digit) gives the room width.

Step 6: Room Type and Features (D12)

Roll (D12) Room Type Description
1 Empty Room - An empty space with no notable features, though an uneasy feeling lingers.
2 Treasure Room - A space filled with valuable items, possibly protected by a trap or defense.
3 Trap Room - The room contains hidden dangers that require careful navigation.
4 Puzzle Room - A room with puzzles or symbols that must be solved to proceed further.
5 Hostile Presence Room - Hostile entities are stationed here, ready to engage intruders.
6 Storage Room - Supplies, weapons, and armor are present, though some items seem tampered with.
7 Archive Room - Filled with dusty books, ancient records, and potential clues to the dungeon's secrets.
8 Laboratory - Strange potions and experimental devices are scattered around, possibly dangerous.
9 Prison Room - Holds captives who might need help or offer valuable information.
10 Boss Room - A powerful foe resides here, guarding something of great importance.
11 Sacred Site - A place resonating with ancient mystical energy that demands respect.
12 Arcane Chamber - The room is filled with magical artifacts or unfamiliar phenomena.

Step 7: Room Encounters (D8)

Roll (D8) Encounter Description
1 No Encounter - The room is eerily quiet, as if undisturbed for ages.
2 Minor Hostile - A small group of creatures lurk cautiously, watching intruders.
3 Cursed Presence - Haunted objects or restless spirits linger, exuding a malevolent aura.
4 Roaming Entity - Creatures wander aimlessly, seemingly unaware of your presence.
5 Environmental Hazard - Dangerous terrain, such as sharp debris or slick surfaces, complicates navigation.
6 Major Hostile - A strong enemy group poses a significant challenge for intruders.
7 Ambush - Hidden foes lie in wait, ready to strike when the moment is right.
8 Elite Threat - A formidable guardian stands ready, protecting something of great value.

Step 8: Room Modifiers (D20)

Roll (D20) Modifier Description
1 Double Trouble - Twice as many enemies as expected, significantly increasing danger.
2 Echoes of the Past - Strange visions or ghostly whispers fill the air, unsettling all who enter.
3 Shifting Walls - The room layout subtly shifts, altering paths and exits unpredictably.
4 Cursed Object - Any treasure found here is cursed, posing a risk to those who take it.
5 Deep Darkness - The room is shrouded in magical darkness, greatly impairing visibility.
6 Empowered Ground - The ground pulses with energy, affecting everyone within, positively or negatively.
7 False Safety - Seemingly harmless objects in the room hide potential dangers, threatening those who are careless.
8 Arcane Instability - Magical energy flickers unpredictably, causing strange and erratic effects.
9 Mysterious Figure - A shadowy figure watches from the corner, its intentions unclear.
10 Guardian Presence - A sentinel or spirit guards the treasure or exit vigilantly.
11 Enhanced Focus - A strange energy sharpens the mind, granting temporary clarity or insight.
12 Illusions - Misleading images or sounds obscure the true layout of the room, making navigation challenging.
13 Strange Markings - Glowing runes cover the walls, hinting at hidden knowledge or a trap.
14 Dimensional Anomaly - The room overlaps with another plane, causing disorientation.
15 Collapsing Structure - Parts of the ceiling or walls crumble, creating a hazard.
16 Temporal Distortion - Time behaves oddly here, making movements feel sluggish or unnaturally fast.
17 Unexpected Aid - A hidden stash or a helpful spirit appears to aid the party.
18 Mimicry - An object or part of the room is a mimic, waiting to attack.
19 Blessing or Inspiration - A positive aura grants the party a sense of purpose and strength.
20 Chaotic Event - Roll D8 and D12 again to introduce an unpredictable twist.

Step 9: Special Events

  • If matching numbers are rolled on the D20, D8, or D12, roll D8 again and add that result to the room encounter.
  • If all three (D20, D8, and D12) match, roll the D8 three times and add all results to the encounter.

Progressing Through the Dungeon

Choose an exit and repeat the steps, drawing hallways and rooms based on the dice rolls. Avoid placing doors at the edge of the map; treat them as solid walls.

Ending the Game

The game ends when the entire grid is filled, a major objective is completed, or players decide to end the session.
